# Psychological & Medical Frameworks in FutureSelf

FutureSelf is an educational simulation coaching tool designed to inspire healthier lifestyle adoptions through data visualization and empathy-driven AI interaction. While not a diagnostic medical device, its underlying prompts and logic rely on established psychological paradigms and general evidence-based medical consensus.

This document outlines the clinical and psychological frameworks utilized throughout the application to guide the AI's logic, reasoning, and user interaction.

## 1. Psychological Frameworks

The core application "Wellbeing Advisor" is explicitly instructed to act as a supportive, empathetic, and evidence-based Personal Wellbeing Advisor utilizing the following clinical psychology frameworks:

### Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
CBT principles are employed to help the user identify and reframe negative thought patterns related to their health and lifestyle decisions.
*   **Cognitive Restructuring:** The AI helps the user identify "cognitive distortions" (e.g., all-or-nothing thinking regarding diet, catastrophizing about heart health).
*   **Socratic Questioning:** The Advisor uses guided questioning to test the validity of the user's anxious thoughts.
*   **Alternative Perspectives:** It encourages the development of balanced, alternative perspectives to reduce anxiety and promote healthy behavioral change.

### Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)
DBT principles are used specifically for emotion regulation and distress tolerance, bridging the gap between momentary emotional spikes and long-term health adherence.
*   **Validation:** The AI is strictly instructed to acknowledge the user's feelings first ("DBT validation") before moving into any problem-solving steps.
*   **Mindfulness:** Reminding the user to stay present and observe their emotions without immediate judgment, particularly helpful when logging negative moods or food binges.
*   **Distress Tolerance:** Providing grounding exercises or "TIPP" skills during moments of high stress or crisis.
*   **Emotion Regulation:** Assisting the user in labeling their emotions to understand their somatic function.

## 2. Medical & Physiological Frameworks

The application's predictive simulations, clinical reports, and biomarker analyses are grounded in general clinical guidelines, primarily mapping the intersection of lifestyle and physiology.

### Mind-Body Connection (Psychoneuroimmunology)
A core tenet of the application is mapping the user's psychological state to their physiological health.
*   **Systemic Inflammation:** The AI connects emotional states (like chronic stress logged by the user) to physical outcomes (like elevated cortisol, heart stress, and systemic cellular inflammation).
*   **Somatic Impact Assessment:** AI analysis explicitly rates the "Impact on Brain", "Impact on Heart", and "Impact on Arteries" when logging both meals and mood states.

### Clinical Reporting Standards
When the AI generates the "Clinical Holistic Report & Forecast", it is system-instructed to:
*   Adopt the persona of a "clinical synthesis AI writing for medical professionals."
*   Use professional medical terminology suitable for a primary care doctor or specialist.
*   Generate an "Action Plan Support" note, explicitly providing a brief note to the user's healthcare provider (HCP) on the AI-generated action plan the user is pursuing, highlighting areas where the HCP might want to offer medical supervision.

### Preventative Cardiology Guidelines (AHA references)
While explicitly generalized to avoid acting as a diagnostic device, the application's underlying logic models cardiovascular trajectories based off established preventative models (such as those outlined by the American Heart Association (AHA) and World Health Organization (WHO)).
*   The system evaluates inputs like Total Cholesterol, HDL, LDL, Fasting Glucose (HbA1c), and Blood Pressure against patient age, smoking status, and activity levels.
*   It visualizes outcomes like "arterial plaque buildup" or "heart muscle strain" correlated to these specific metabolic markers.

## 3. Behavioral Design (Gamification)

The application utilizes concepts of behavioral reinforcement to encourage habit forming.
*   **Operant Conditioning:** Positive reinforcement is applied via "Earned Health Time". Healthy actions (logging a meal with a high health score, entering a positive/low-stress mood, completing a daily quest) immediately reward the user with "+0.5h" or "+1.0h" to their visual lifespan.
*   **Loss Aversion:** Conversely, highly poor dietary choices visibly deduct health time, demonstrating the immediate consequence of negative behaviors.
*   **Action Planning:** Creating 30-day "Algorithm" plans broken down into weekly, focused steps to prevent overwhelming the user, establishing incremental behavioral changes.
